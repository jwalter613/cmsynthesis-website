# CM Synthesis — Domain Name Research

**Brand:** CM Synthesis
**Company site:** cmsynthesis.[tld]
**Tone:** Dark, minimal, premium. Teenage Engineering meets Elektron.
**Audience:** Musicians, producers, hardware enthusiasts, DIY synth builders
**Status:** Research only — do NOT register anything yet

**Locked constraint:** No "alpine" in the URL. Domain represents the company (CM Synthesis), not the product (Alpine). Scales if a second product is added.

---

## Domain Candidates

### 1. `cmsynthesis.com`
**Reasoning:** The company name, spelled out. Clean, professional, unambiguous. This is the URL that goes on the back panel, in the manual, and in every press mention. Scales to future products — if Alpine is followed by a second instrument, the domain still makes sense.
**Availability:** Moderate — "synthesis" domains are popular but this specific combination is obscure enough to likely be available. Check Porkbun first.
**Recommended TLD:** `.com`

---

### 2. `cmsynthesis.audio`
**Reasoning:** The `.audio` TLD is increasingly recognized in music tech circles and immediately signals what the company makes. Strong personality — Teenage Engineering-adjacent energy. A credible alternative if `.com` is taken, and worth buying as a redirect regardless.
**Availability:** High — `.audio` registrations are sparse. Very likely available.
**Est. cost:** ~$15–20/yr at Porkbun

---

### 3. `cmsynthesis.io`
**Reasoning:** `.io` carries a maker/open-source hardware association — popular with DIY electronics projects and open hardware companies. Fits the open firmware + open STL ethos of CMS Alpine well. Slightly more expensive than `.com`.
**Availability:** High.
**Est. cost:** ~$30–35/yr at Porkbun

---

### 4. `cmsynthesis.fm`
**Reasoning:** `.fm` is the ccTLD for Micronesia, widely used by music and audio brands as a clever play on FM radio. Has personality and niche recognition. Slightly premium cost.
**Availability:** High.
**Est. cost:** ~$35/yr at Porkbun

## Hosting Recommendation

**Vercel** — `vercel.com`

- Free tier is genuinely excellent for a static marketing site
- Connects directly to a GitHub repository — push code, site deploys automatically in ~30 seconds
- Global CDN — fast load times everywhere, no configuration needed
- Custom domain support on free tier (some competitors charge for this)
- HTTPS automatic via Let's Encrypt
- Zero-config — drop in an `index.html` and it just works
- Note: free tier is for non-commercial use per Vercel's terms — upgrade to Pro (~$20/mo) once units are shipping

**Setup workflow:**
1. Push the website files to a GitHub repository
2. Connect the repo to Vercel (one-time OAuth)
3. Add custom domain in Vercel dashboard
4. Update DNS at Porkbun to point at Vercel's nameservers (or add CNAME record)
5. Done — auto-deploys on every push to main

No server to manage. No WordPress to update. No hosting bills. Right-sized for where this project is now.
